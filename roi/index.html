What this means — the right framing
This is not a headcount reduction. Today the team has 4.5 QA engineers operating at ~5% of their real QA capacity — almost all their time goes to manual, repetitive work. The recovered capacity means those same engineers can finally do the work the business needs: coverage on high-risk projects, exploratory testing, and AI-driven automation.

The alternative framing: without these improvements, Meltwater would need to hire 4–5 additional QA engineers to achieve equivalent coverage. Instead, that capacity is unlocked from within the existing team through process and tooling improvements.

For the two most critical projects — GenAI Lens (risk 2.4 High) and Mira (risk 2.3 High) — the program targets moving both to Medium risk by end of Q2, directly reducing the probability of critical bugs reaching end users.
